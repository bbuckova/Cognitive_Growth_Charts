Methodology

Our normative models were developed using advanced statistical techniques including:

• Generalized Additive Models (GAMs): For capturing non-linear age effects

• Multiple Regression: For modeling demographic predictors

• Quantile Regression: For generating percentile-based norms

• Cross-validation: For model validation and parameter optimization

Data Sources

The normative data is derived from a comprehensive dataset of cognitive assessments from healthy participants across multiple research sites. All data has been anonymized and aggregated to protect participant privacy while maintaining statistical power.

Sample Characteristics:

• Age range: 18-90 years

• Education: 8-25 years

• Gender balanced samples

• Multiple cultural and linguistic backgrounds

Model Validation

Each normative model underwent rigorous validation using:

• Cross-validation: 10-fold cross-validation for all models

• Independent test sets: 20% holdout samples for final validation

• External validation: Testing on independent research cohorts

• Clinical validation: Comparison with known clinical groups

Chart Descriptions

Age vs Performance: Displays the relationship between participant age and raw cognitive performance scores. Shows age-related changes and identifies optimal age ranges for each measure.

Education vs Performance: Illustrates how years of formal education correlate with cognitive performance, helping account for educational effects in normative scoring.

Model Residuals: Shows the residuals from the fitted normative model, helping identify potential outliers or model limitations. Well-fitted models show randomly distributed residuals.

Predicted vs Actual: Compares model predictions against observed scores. Points along the diagonal indicate good model fit, while deviations suggest areas for model improvement.
